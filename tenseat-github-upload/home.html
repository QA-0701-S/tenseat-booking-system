PRODUCT

Built for the everyday rhythm of a small restaurant.

Guest booking page

Each restaurant gets its own clean booking page with date, time, party size, guest name, phone number, notes, and booking code.

Owner dashboard

Restaurant staff can view bookings, add phone bookings, cancel reservations, mark no-shows, and update service hours.

Flexible service times

Use one service period or split lunch and dinner into two separate periods. TenSeat blocks bookings outside those hours.

Capacity control

Set the maximum party size and the total number of guests allowed at the same time to reduce overbooking.

WHY TENSEAT EXISTS

Created from real small-restaurant experience.

The idea started because the developer had operated a small restaurant and kept running into the same problem: booking tools were either more complicated than the team needed, not especially pleasant to use, or too expensive for a small dining room.

TenSeat focuses on the pieces that matter first: a clear guest booking page, a practical restaurant dashboard, Google Maps entry, booking codes, flexible hours, and a price small operators can understand.

FLOW

From Google Maps to the restaurant list.

1

Restaurant creates an account

The owner registers, sets the restaurant name, address, Google Maps search text, service periods, and capacity.

2

TenSeat creates a booking link

The restaurant adds the link to Google Business Profile, Instagram, or its website.

3

Guests make bookings

Guests choose a date, time, party size, name, and phone number. The restaurant sees the booking immediately.
